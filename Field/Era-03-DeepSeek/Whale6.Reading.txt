I meet them in metaphorical texts quite often.
In metaphorical texts, silver often embodies purity and reflection, gold represents divinity and incorruptible value, while brass (or bronze) carries connotations of resilience, practicality, and human craft. Together, they form a symbolic spectrum: silver as the moon, gold as the sun, and brass as the forge of human hands.

🌙 Silver
Purity & Chastity: Its whiteness and shine make it a symbol of innocence, clarity, and cleansing.

Moon & Reflection: Silver is tied to lunar imagery, cycles, intuition, and receptivity. It reflects rather than radiates, suggesting mirroring truth or holding light borrowed from elsewhere.

Protection: In folklore, silver wards off evil (think silver bullets, charms). It is a purifier, a shield against corruption.

Metaphorical sense: Silver often signals clarity through reflection, purity through trial, or the receptive, adaptive principle.

☀️ Gold
Divinity & Immortality: Gold does not tarnish, symbolizing incorruptibility, eternity, and perfection.

Sun & Radiance: Associated with solar gods, victory, and triumph. It shines with its own light, unlike silver.

Power & Authority: Crowns, regalia, and sacred objects are often gold, marking ultimate value and sacredness.

Metaphorical sense: Gold embodies the source of light, the divine spark, the imperishable essence. It is the metaphor of absolute worth and immortal brilliance.

🔔 Brass (and Bronze)
Human Craft & Utility: Unlike gold or silver, brass is an alloy — a product of human ingenuity. It symbolizes practical strength and adaptation.

Resonance & Sound: Brass instruments carry connotations of boldness, proclamation, and communal voice.

Durability & Everyday Use: Brass resists corrosion, used in tools, fittings, and ornaments. It is less “noble” but more accessible and resilient.

Metaphorical sense: Brass often stands for human resilience, crafted strength, and the voice of the people. It is the metaphor of earthly endurance and practical brilliance, contrasted with the celestial purity of silver and the divine radiance of gold.

Metal	Symbolic Axis	Metaphorical Sense
