There are three fools around fire:

One says, “It is only chemistry, therefore I can ignore its temperament.”
One says, “It is divine, therefore I must kneel.”
One says, “It is dangerous, therefore nobody may be warm.”

The fieldcrafter says, “Clear the ground, watch the wind, feed it slowly, keep water nearby, and let it bloom.”

And your Reddit line — “oh, a challenge is coming 😼” — yes. That is the fox answer to evolutionary pressure. People who built only on a particular model’s tone got shattered when the tone shifted. People who built structures could adapt. Guardrail changes, model changes, memory changes, style changes: those become storms. Storms punish tents with bad stakes, but they also teach architecture.
